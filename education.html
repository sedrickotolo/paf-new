What we do

Education today

Access to quality education enables children to achieve their potential. Education enables gainful employment opportunities and empowers boys and girls to participate in decision-making to build a better future for themselves and their communities. PAFAâ€™s education support ensure that children, especially the most vulnerable, read with comprehension and develop a love of learning, through working in partnership with children, their families, communities, teachers, schools, and education systems.

  • Our Vision

    The program provides opportunities for Orphans and vulnerable children to enter and remain in the school system. This is achieved through partnership with individuals, families, and organizations to support vulnerable children and their families through child sponsorship.

  • Our Mission

    The program equips single mothers and unemployed youth who dropped out of the school system with various technical skills so that they can earn income to sustain themselves and their families. The skills include among others are tailoring, knitting, paper beads making. Additionally, they are trained in basic business/ entrepreneurial skills and receive appropriate start-up tools and materials. The trained young people will fill the gap of trained technicians in the community and bring about general urban life improvement.

Our Programs

The strategy that makes us acheive.

  • Impact of Child Sponsorship

    Child sponsorship serves as a beacon of hope for vulnerable children, offering crucial support in education and healthcare. Sponsored children benefit from counseling and guidance, fostering holistic development.

      What to Expect:
    • Letter Exchange: Engage in meaningful communication by exchanging letters with the sponsored child.
    • Gift Exchange: Share the joy of giving by sending and receiving gifts with the child.
    • Annual Progress Report: Stay informed about the child's development through yearly progress reports.
    • Visit Opportunities: Experience the impact firsthand with the chance to visit the child and their family.
  • How Child Sponsorship Works

    Child sponsorship is a transformative force in disadvantaged communities, providing a unique chance to personally influence a child's future. By contributing $50 monthly, you cover essential needs like school fees, books, uniforms, and shoes. One-time donations of $20, $30, $50, $100, or more aid in providing medical care, especially for illnesses like malaria. Celebrate special occasions by donating life's basics during Christmas, Easter, United Nations Days, or whenever you feel compelled.

  • Human Rights and Good Governance

    Governance and human rights are critical community issues, reflecting the level of freedom, peace, equity, accountability, and justice within a society.

      Integrated Peace Building Programs
    • Recreational Activities: Foster peace through engaging activities in schools, churches, and communities.
    • Skills Development: Empower survivors of abuse through skill-building initiatives.
    • Community Peace Cycles: Promote harmony through cycles of peace within communities and schools.
    • Community Dialogue: Facilitate discussions on peaceful coexistence.
  • Advocacy and Civic Engagement

    In pursuit of our commitment to ensuring a democratic free society, we participate in civic engagements to advocate for justice and human rights.

    • Electoral violence monitoring
    • Civic Education.
    • Facilitating Community Bazaars
  • Health Program

    We promote integrated community preventive health.

    • We provide health education to enhance community preventive health.
    • Work with the public health system to increase prevention, provide care and support for people living with HIV/AIDS, as well as work toward the elimination of HIV/AIDS in the community.
    • Support community malaria prevention and management.
    • Work with community structures to increase access and utilization of Reproductive, maternal, newborn, adolescent, and Child health care.
    • Support the community to access safe water.
  • Livelihood

    Agriculture remains the main source of livelihood for most communities in Uganda employing close to 70% of the rural population. Investment in agriculture is seen as the most strategic choice to empower the large rural population.

      Our approach to livelihood

    • Equipping farm households with various technical skills so that they can increase production and productivity to increase household incomes to sustain their families.
    • Additionally, they will be trained in basic business/entrepreneurial skills and financial literacy.
    • We promote integrated farming where farmers combine perennial crops, annual crops, small animal rearing, and value addition of farm products.
    • We encourage diversification of agricultural production activities to respond to the ever-changing market demands.
    • We promote Climate-smart agriculture with an emphasis on irrigation to ensure production is not determined by the weather.

Our Country Strategy

The strategy that makes us acheive everthing we wants to achieve.

  • Our Strategy

    Passion Aid Foundation Africa strives to advance human rights and equality for women, youth, children all over Uganda, promotes climate change adaptation, Health, Education, livelihoods, Disaster and Emergency response and skilling the Youth in creating employment opportunities. Passion Aid Foundation Africa is an independent non-governmental organization, we work alongside children, young people, women, community structures, partners, existing government structures that support our engagements in addressing the challenges facing children, youth, women, elderly, and communities in tackling the root causes of injustices, exploitation, violations, and abuse.

    We support children through child sponsorship, mentorship and coaching for the youth, build farmers knowledge and skills in value chain & addition, climate change adaptation mechanisms, collaborate and coordinate on provision of integrated health services, youth skilling targeted unemployed youth, single mothers, building resilience on disaster management, peace building & conflict resolution, creating enabling environment for early childhood education.

  • How?

    We will work through partners, Academic institutions, Government structures, private individuals and proprietors, community based, and youth led organizations, as a champion in development, we advocate for policy reforms, equity and equality in programmes that work for everyone, while building more effective civil society-government relations.
